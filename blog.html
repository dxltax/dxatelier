Why Frontend Developers Should Care About Accessibility

Category: Opinions | Date: May 7, 2025

Accessibility isn’t just a “nice to have”—it’s a responsibility. The web is for everyone, and that means your product should be usable by all, including those with disabilities. You don't need to be a specialist to care. Start small: use semantic HTML, label your forms, ensure color contrast is readable. It’s part of writing good code.

Beyond ethics, it’s also good UX. A screen reader-friendly form is often also more usable to the average user. And guess what? Google loves accessible pages too. SEO boost? Yes please.

TL;DR: Make your frontend inclusive. Because you can. Because it matters.

5 VS Code Extensions I Can’t Live Without

Category: Tips | Date: May 1, 2025

Let’s be real—VS Code is already awesome, but with the right extensions? Chef’s kiss. Here are five that supercharged my workflow:

  • Prettier – automatic formatting saves my soul daily.
  • ESLint – code linting FTW.
  • GitLens – view git blame like a god.
  • Tailwind IntelliSense – auto-complete Tailwind classes like a pro.
  • Live Server – real-time browser reloads.

Try them. Your future self (and your repo collaborators) will thank you.
